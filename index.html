Bio

I am currently an MPhil student in Computational Biophysics at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou). My research focuses on computational protein design and biomolecular simulation, with recent interests extending toward AI for Science and diffusion-based generative modeling for molecular dynamics trajectories.

My recent work studies how PETase recognizes PET surfaces through large-scale molecular simulations, trajectory analysis, and rational engineering. I am also exploring how generative models can accelerate long-timescale MD sampling while preserving structural realism, thermodynamic consistency, and kinetic fidelity.

Research

Selected ongoing and recent projects spanning enzyme–polymer recognition, protein engineering, and generative modeling for biomolecular dynamics.

Binding Mechanism and Rational Engineering of IsPETase on PET Surfaces

Jun 2024 – Dec 2025

Coarse-grained molecular simulation · trajectory analysis · protein design

  • Built coarse-grained PET slab models and IsPETase–PET interfacial systems to study enzyme recognition on polymer surfaces.
  • Combined large-scale molecular dynamics, PCA, and trajectory-based conformational analysis to resolve binding pathways and state transitions.
  • Identified productive versus non-productive recognition routes across enzyme variants and proposed design strategies that improved productive binding probability by nearly 20% in simulation.

Generative Modeling of Biomolecular MD Trajectories with Renormalization-Group Diffusion

Jan 2026 – Present

Diffusion models · MD acceleration · kinetic and thermodynamic fidelity

  • Investigating diffusion-based generative models for biomolecular trajectory generation to reduce the cost of long-timescale MD sampling.
  • Reproduced RGDM and EigenFold modules and analyzed their ability to preserve structural realism together with thermodynamic and kinetic fidelity.

Computational Study of the Interplay between Flexibility and Stability in IsPETase for Plastic Degradation

Jul 2023 – May 2024

All-atom MD · docking · umbrella sampling

  • Performed molecular docking of IsPETase with a PET single-chain substrate using AutoDock, followed by all-atom MD simulations in GROMACS.
  • Applied umbrella sampling to calculate the binding free-energy profile of the protein–substrate association process.
